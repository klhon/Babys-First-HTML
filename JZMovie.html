Favorite Director - Quentin Tarantino
and His Movies Ranked

I am a huge film enthusiast. I do not have the right to call myself a nerd yet because I don't know any of the technicalities and terminologies assocaited with film. With that said, I do know when I have watched a good movie. I know when a movie has a good plot, good characters, good effects and/or good writing.
I know that some people consider him overrated, but I personally really like his style. Believe me when I say that I'm not into all the blood, but he uses it appropriately and it's not just a gore fest. I really love all the films he has directed (alone) and it was extremely hard to choose which one I liked most. Here's the finalized list. Please, don't hurt me...

1. Kill Bill Vol. 1 & Vol. 2 (2003-2004)

For me, I LOVE Kill Bill! Not only because there's a strong leading woman (who is played beautifully by Uma Thurman), but also because of the Asian culture that is infused into the movies. In the first movie, the main antagonist is Lucy Liu and the entire movie has a nice blend of Western and samurai elements. Also her Bruce Lee track suit is 10/10.

2. Reservoir Dogs (1992)

This movie is so good! The unique characters really allowed me to fall in love with this movie. Young Tim Roth is oof! Dang! I would smack that as Britney would say. An undercover cop amongst the mafia is a typical Hong Kong movie plot, but Quentin makes this trope refreshing as finding the mole is the driving device for the WHOLE film.

3. Inglourious Basterds (2009)

Christopher Waltz.

4. Pulp Fiction (1994)

I know that this is a really good movie, if not Quentin's best. BUT I watched the movie four times two years in a row. I swear that I would rank this movie higher than 4th place, but I watched it too many times in such a short period of time. I have no idea how anyone can possibly watch a single film monthly.

5. Django Unchained (2012)

This will be the last explanation because I'm tired from this hackathon. I really liked how he used fake blood and wasn't afraid of making commentary on slavery through the use of symbolism. Also, Leo's improvisation was impeccable, but his poor hand! Poor Kerry Washington!

6. Jackie Brown (1997)

7. The Hateful Eight (2015)
